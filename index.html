Projects

Vibrations of an N-gon Mass-Spring System (and Further Geometry)

This is the project I completed in my third year, where I looked at the time-harmonic vibrations of various mass-spring systems. This resulted in some quite beautful animations of the vibrations of the systems. I also extended the simple N-gon geometry moving into more complex ones such as that with concentic circles. A MATLAB app was also developed to allow users to experiment with their own geometries in this framework, and an object oriented approach was leveraged, allowing the easy creation and removing of additional masses. The project was very well received by my supervisor and I was awarded the top mark in the year for it. It was also entered into The Institute of Mathematics and its Applications (IMA) National Best Maths Project competition 2025, where it was awarded first place, resulting in a Mathematics Today article being written about it. The project can be found on my GitHub page.

A Spectral Problem Applied to a Chiral Multi-Structure

This is the project I am currently working on, where we are utilising gyroscopes to model the Coriolis effect experienced due to the rotation on Earth. This is a very interesting project as it could have some interesting geophysical applications, and due to the symmetries of the system, we can formulate it fully analytically, rather than relying on complex meshing. We are currently in the process of writing up the results and hope to submit it to a journal in the near future. The project is written in MATLAB.
